Atlan Offer Wizard

Personalized "Here's why you belong at Atlan" offer ceremony pages, auto-generated from ATS data and synthesized with Claude. Three-act narrative at ~$0.006 per page.

The problem

The offer stage is the highest-stakes moment in the hiring funnel — and most companies treat it like an afterthought. A PDF attachment, a plain-text email, maybe a generic congratulations message. Nothing that makes a candidate feel genuinely seen or signals the quality of the organization they're about to join.

Writing deeply personalized offer narratives for every candidate doesn't scale manually. But the candidate experience at the offer stage is exactly where effort pays off — it's the difference between a signed offer and a declined one.

What I built

A system that automatically generates personalized "offer ceremony" pages for every candidate who reaches the offer stage at Atlan. Each page tells a three-act story:

  • "Who you are" — Synthesized from real interview feedback — what interviewers found impressive, the candidate's background, their strengths. Personal, specific, never generic.
  • "Why you belong at Atlan" — Maps the candidate's strengths to Atlan's values and the role's impact. Makes the case for fit in a way that's unique to them.
  • "Our offer" — The formal offer details, presented with context and warmth rather than cold numbers.

The system triggers automatically when a candidate moves to the offer stage in our ATS. It pulls their full interview history, synthesizes the narrative with Claude, generates a premium web page, and notifies the TA team with a shareable link — all hands-free.

Impact

  • Every candidate gets a personalized page — No manual writing, no bottleneck on the TA team, no "we'll get to it later"
  • Costs ~$0.006 per page — Effectively free at any hiring volume
  • Tone calibrates by seniority — A senior engineering hire gets a more intellectually dense narrative than an early-career hire
  • Print-ready — Designed to look as good on paper as on screen, so it can be used as a physical artifact too
  • Signals organizational quality — When a candidate receives a page like this, it tells them something about the kind of company Atlan is

Why it matters

Most TA teams are so focused on sourcing and interviewing that the offer experience gets neglected. This tool closes that gap — turning a transactional moment into a memorable one, at scale, with zero ongoing effort.
